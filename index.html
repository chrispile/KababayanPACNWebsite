About PACN

Pilipinx-American Culture Night, PACN, is an annual production by Kababayan at UCI held at the Bren Events Center. It is Kababayan's largest event, as it represents various issues that affect the Pilipinx-American communities. Through the art of theater and traditional Pilipinx culture dance, a message is woven that will captivate the audience and present issues that are relevant to today's Pilipinx-American experiences.

Due to the shifting nature of Pilipinx-American identity, each of our culture nights is a unique production that reflects the experiences and hard work of every cast member. Aside from the cast members, the community at UCI and audience members are given a chance to learn and enjoy the dances and the singing of the choir, and explore the issues in the narrative of our skit. Through the magic of PACN, the cast and audience are exposed to different Pilipinx-American issues, traditions, culture, and history.

About Kababayan at UCI

Kababayan at UCI promotes awareness and enrichment of the Pilipino(a) culture, tradition, and heritage within the community of UCI and the greater Orange County area. Since 1974, Kababayan emphasizes the social, cultural, political, academic, and community aspects of the Pilipino experience.

Kababayan is one of the largest collegiate Pilipino-American organizations in California, and one of the largest in the nation. In past years, we’ve reached over 600 members. Intimidating as it may seem, each Kaba member is recognized for their special talents and personality. True to its name, we are members of a large extended family, making our lives here at UCI more involved, educational, supportive, exciting, and most of all, more fun. Kababayan is its members.

Kababayan is here for you. It is here to nurture you as a leader, present you with valuable friendships, introduce you to all facets of the real world, and provide you with a second family away from home. Do not be afraid to take the initiative to come to the meetings and claim yourself as “kababayan.” We were all in your shoes at one point in time. So on that note, we welcome you to our organization, our home, and our family.

Maria Clara Suite originated during the Philippine’s Spanish colonial era. Along with them came European dances but the addition of the addition of their native style allowed them to claim these dances as their own. The suite brings to life the elegance and charm of the mestiza Filipina, as well as the gallantry and boldness of the mestizo Filipino. These dances usually tell a story about courtship, love, and flirtation.

Kaba Modern was established in 1992 by Arnel Calvario and is the collegiate division of the KM Kollective.He founded the group to perform in the modern dance suite of Kaba’s annual PCN (Pilipino cultural night). The group has since evolved to be one of the most cutting edge Hip Hop dance crews in CA. Kaba Modern’s legacy has lived on as a family of artists while embracing diverse styles of dance to create a name for themselves in the dance community.

"Moro" is the term for the population of Muslims in the Philippines. They are mostly found in Mindanao and their culture is heavily Malay-influenced. Their dances are marked by intricate hand and arm movements to the beat of the kulintang (a gong), shimmering costumes, and embody mysticism, royalty, and beauty. Singkil, Pangalay, and Kapa Malong Malong are examples of well-known Moro dances.

Rural is a dance suite that focuses on the Pilipinx traditional dances originating in the Christian lowlands of the Philippines. The dances typically embody celebration usually surrounding a common theme: the joys and simplicities of life. All the dances involve bright colors, and a light hearted environment to express the love of work, music, and life. Tinikling, one of the most iconic traditional dance of the Philippines, is part of this suite.

Senior Suite is a modern hip-hop dance collective that is exclusively open to the current graduating class. This suite offers the opportunity for seniors to commemorate their final year here at UC Irvine and celebrate their very last time participating in PACN.

Filipino Tribal Dance is a form of dancing that originated from the Southern Region of the Philippines, primarily the tribes of Mindanao. Traditional tribal dance reflect the environments of the tribes within these regions and consists of very graceful dance moves that imitate the movement of animals such as birds and fish. Most dances of tribal were performed for the ‘Gods’ and this form of dancing is very intertwined closely with ceremonials, rituals, and sacrifice.
